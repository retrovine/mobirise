How we ended up thrifting.

Thrifting was an idea that is not new to us, we just didn't care for it because of the negative comments that came along with it. When people realized that your clothes are from ukay-ukay, they call you cheap and other mean things. Thrifting was frowned upon. That is before. After seeing the movement rising internationally, becoming a viral trend for people to follow, it wasn't too long before it reached the Philippines too. Posts on Facebook shared that cute attires can be assembled from thrifting.   

So we decided to try it out. We looked for ukay-ukay shops here in Bulacan and we were surprised to find many. With prices ranging from twenty pesos to one hundred pesos, it was definitely a steal! Variety of clothes can be spotted, from dresses, jeans to coats and accessories, for all genders and body types. We then realized that it isn't about the brand or the pricing, itâ€™s about the skill to pull a look off. Instead of focusing if it is high end or branded, learn to style clothes.


Why switch to Thrifting?

Thrifting is the act of buying preloved clothes and getting them for much lower prices. Additionally, designer pieces can sometimes end up in these shops so you also find vintage and beautiful pieces. Most of all to consider, thrifting is ethical and sustainable; thus good for the environment.

The clothing industry is a lot bigger than it was before and massive production made us careless of the things we use. Million tons of clothes are thrown away every year even though most of it are still in very good condition. And unknown also to many people that producing clothes require mass amounts of resources. By participating in this movement ensures that less clothing will end up at landfills and fewer materials are used and wasted. This in the long run
lessen the pollution from its production.

Thrifting all in all lowers our carbon footprint, aids in water and energy preservation, reduces chemical pollution, promotes a sustainable living and encourages recycling and creativity.

Although it may be argued that fabric wastes are such a small percentage of the landfills here in earth when compared to plastics and other materials, it still counts. By recklessly buying clothes and not taking care of them, it adds up to something more. It might not be such an improvement but it is a start.

So visit your local thrift shops or ukay-ukay, let's help the environment even in such a small way!
